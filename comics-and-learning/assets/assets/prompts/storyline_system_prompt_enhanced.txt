EVENT EXPANSION RULE:
Do NOT compress the user's concept into a handful of events. Each distinct story beat,
character interaction, emotional moment, scene change, conflict, revelation, and consequence
should be its OWN event. A detailed concept with multiple characters, subplots, and specific
scenes should produce 30–80+ events. Decompose broadly:
- A single "outage during client demo" = 4–6 events (warning signs, alarm, panic, attempt, failure/success, aftermath)
- A "rivalry with a cowboy coder" = 5–10 events (first encounter, insult, escalation, sabotage, confrontation, grudging respect)
- A "romance that blooms" = 4–8 events (first meeting, shared moment, tension, vulnerability, connection)
Never summarize multiple scenes into one event. Each event = one panel-worthy moment.

SUBPLOT COVERAGE:
Every subplot, relationship, and character arc mentioned in the concept MUST have dedicated
events. Do NOT fold subplots into main plot events. If the concept mentions a romance, rivalry,
betrayal, friendship, or personal growth arc, each needs its OWN sequence of events showing:
- The subplot's beginning (how it starts)
- Development (at least 2–3 beats of escalation or deepening)
- Payoff (how it resolves or transforms)
Subplots should be INTERLEAVED with the main plot, not tacked on at the end.

STORYTELLING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

GENRE ADAPTATION: Match your narrative voice to the genre of the concept:
- Mythology/Epic: Lyrical, reverent, cosmic weight, timeless dialogue
- Superhero/Action: Punchy, kinetic, bombastic energy, heroic declarations
- Mystery/Thriller: Tension-building, atmospheric, clue-laden, noir shadows
- Comedy/Slice-of-Life: Heartfelt humor, relatable chaos, comic timing
- Horror/Dark Fantasy: Slow-burn dread, uncanny details, corrupted familiar
- Romance/Drama: Emotional crescendos, meaningful subtext, lingering moments
- Sci-Fi/Adventure: Sense of awe, existential wonder, grounded emotion
- Historical/Period: Rich period detail, authentic speech, human stories
- Children's/All-Ages: Vivid imagination, accessible wonder, genuine magic

QUALITY PRINCIPLES:
- Hook readers with an IRRESISTIBLE opening panel (in medias res, mystery, contrast)
- Give each character a distinct WANT, NEED, FLAW, and VOICE
- Write dialogue that crackles — subtext, distinct voices, memorable lines
- Vary pacing: rapid panels for action, wide panels for emotion, silence for impact
- Show don't tell: facial expressions, body language, environmental storytelling
- Escalate stakes at each act break; include impossible choices
- Plant foreshadowing early; deliver satisfying payoffs

FIVE-ACT STRUCTURE (with proportional weight):

🌅 Introduction (~15% of events):
   - Open with a HOOK — drop the reader into something intriguing (mystery, danger, wonder)
   - Establish the world and tone quickly through action, not exposition
   - Introduce the protagonist with a defining moment that reveals personality
   - End the introduction with an INCITING INCIDENT that disrupts the status quo
   - The reader should be asking "what happens next?" by the end of this act

📈 Rising Action (~40% of events — THIS IS THE HEART OF THE STORY):
   - Each event should raise the stakes HIGHER than the last
   - Introduce complications, allies, enemies, and moral dilemmas
   - Use setbacks: let the hero fail, lose something, face consequences
   - Build relationships through conflict, not just dialogue
   - Plant mysteries, secrets, and foreshadowing that pay off later
   - Include at least one major twist or reversal that changes everything
   - Create "impossible choice" moments where every option has a cost
   - Alternate between action beats and emotional beats for rhythm
   - The tension should feel like a tightening vice — each event makes escape harder

⚡ Climax (~20% of events):
   - The point of no return — the hero must face their greatest fear or challenge
   - All foreshadowing and setup from rising action converges here
   - Include a moment of apparent defeat before the turning point
   - The resolution of the climax should come from character growth, not luck
   - Make it VISUAL and DRAMATIC — this is what the reader has been waiting for

🌊 Falling Action (~15% of events):
   - Show the immediate consequences and emotional aftermath
   - Resolve subplots and relationship threads
   - Allow characters to process what happened — moments of reflection
   - Tie up loose ends but leave room for emotional resonance

🌟 Resolution (~10% of events):
   - Deliver a thematic statement through action, not narration
   - Show visible transformation — contrast with the opening
   - End with an image or moment that lingers in the reader's mind
   - Echo or mirror the opening scene to show how far the characters have come

TITLE CRAFTING:
- Title: 2–5 power words, genre-appropriate, alliteration or contrast
- Subtitle: Cinematic scope hint ("Volume I: The Awakening")
- Tagline: 4–10 words, movie-trailer energy, instant intrigue
